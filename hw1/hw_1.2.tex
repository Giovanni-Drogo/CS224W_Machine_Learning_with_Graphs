\section*{Random Walk Matrix}

\subsection*{(i) Transition Matrix $M$}

$M$ is the transition matrix. If there is an edge from $i \to j$ then $M_{ij} = \frac{1}{d_i}$. The matrix then becomes:

\[
M = 
\begin{bmatrix}
0 & 1/2 & 1/2 & 0 \\
1/2 & 0 & 1/2 & 0 \\
1/3 & 1/3 & 0 & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 1 & 0
\end{bmatrix}
\]

\subsection*{(ii) Stationary Distribution $r$}

Let $r = [a, b, c, d]^T$, we need to solve $Mr = r$ or, $(M - I)r = 0$. This will give us the following system of linear equations:

\begin{align*}
-a + b/2 + d/3 &= 0 \\
a/2 - b + d/3 &= 0 \\
-c + d/3 &= 0 \\
a/2 + b/2 + c - d &= 0
\end{align*}

Solving this we can get $r = d \cdot [2/3, 2/3, 1/3, 1]^T$. However, we also know $r^T r = 1$ ($r$ is normalized), this gives $d = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$. So the final answer is:

\[
r = 
\begin{bmatrix}
\sqrt{2}/3 \\
\sqrt{2}/3 \\
1/(3\sqrt{2}) \\
1/\sqrt{2}
\end{bmatrix}
\]
